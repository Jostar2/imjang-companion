# GTM.md

## Positioning

Do not position this as a general real-estate app.

Position it as:

- a buyer-side due diligence workspace
- a field-visit comparison and reporting system
- a replacement for scattered notes, gallery photos, and spreadsheets

## Priority channels

### 1. Founder-led outbound

Target:

- buyer agents
- small investor operators
- diligence-heavy property scouts

Message:

- stop losing visit context
- deliver same-day structured reports
- compare properties with stored rationale, not memory

### 2. Content and search

Topics:

- imjang checklist templates
- property comparison frameworks
- field visit red-flag guides
- report examples for buyers and investors

Purpose:

- acquire individual buyers
- collect language that sharpens the ICP
- build credibility for outbound outreach

### 3. Partner distribution

Potential partners:

- buyer consultants
- home inspection professionals
- renovation or repair reviewers

This is a later channel, not the first one.

## Launch funnel

1. landing page visit
2. waitlist or demo request
3. interview or concierge pilot
4. activated workspace
5. paid buyer pack or paid monthly plan
6. shared report
7. referral or team expansion

## Key messages

- "Turn field visits into structured decisions."
- "Compare properties from evidence, not memory."
- "Capture once in the field, deliver a report the same day."

## Early experiments

- interview 10 buyers, 10 buyer agents, and 10 investor operators
- publish one landing page with two pricing paths: buyer pack vs. team workflow
- run at least three concierge pilots before self-serve launch
- measure which persona reaches report generation fastest

## Metrics

- landing page to waitlist rate
- waitlist to interview rate
- interview to pilot rate
- pilot to paid rate
- activation rate to first completed visit
- report share rate

## Failure conditions

- users only browse listings and never complete visits
- users complete visits but never open comparison or report
- agents see value but will not pay because report output is not client-grade
