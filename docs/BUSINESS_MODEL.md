# BUSINESS_MODEL.md

## Core thesis

This repository now carries two linked product theses:

1. `Imjang Companion`
   A buyer-side due diligence workspace for structured field visits, comparison, and reporting.
2. `Codex Launch OS`
   A Codex-managed delivery system that can move from brief to launch-ready product with bounded agents, QA, release gates, and operating artifacts.

The vertical app is the lighthouse product. The launch system is the reusable engine.

## Recommended monetization order

### 1. Primary business model: buyer-agent-first B2B2C SaaS

Sell the workflow first to one repeated-use operator profile:

- independent buyer agents who run repeated weekly visits and need same-day client-ready summaries

Keep these as second-wave personas after the buyer-agent motion is proven:

- small investor teams
- research assistants or partner reviewers
- boutique acquisition or diligence teams

Let those operators generate client-facing or decision-facing outputs:

- shareable visit reports
- comparison summaries
- branded review packets

This is stronger than a pure consumer subscription because the professional user has repeated workflow frequency and clearer ROI.

### Why buyer agent first

- weekly visit frequency is easier to observe than one-off buyer behavior
- the same operator often captures the visit and sends the summary, so onboarding is simpler
- the value story is concrete: faster report turnaround, less note loss, cleaner client follow-up
- the first paid pilot can stay single-operator and avoid team-collaboration scope creep

### 2. Secondary business model: transaction-based consumer pack

Use individual buyers as an acquisition wedge:

- one project or one purchase-cycle workspace
- limited duration access
- report export or share-link included

This is useful for validation and cash collection, but it should not be the main recurring revenue engine.

### 3. Later business model: partner or referral revenue

Possible later extensions:

- home inspection or repair review referrals
- mortgage or financing partner referrals
- moving or relocation partners

Do not lead with this. The product needs trust before monetizing the decision flow with referrals.

## Why the primary model is B2B2C

- The repo's domain model centers on repeated field capture and report generation, not listing discovery.
- Optional collaborator and shareable report hints point toward a professional operator plus report recipient structure.
- The workflow value compounds with repetition: templates, comparison logic, historical visits, and report speed matter more for pros than for one-time buyers.

## Strategic decision

Treat `individual buyers` as validation and top-of-funnel users.

Treat `independent buyer agents` as the first recurring revenue customer.

Treat `small investor teams` as the next expansion segment once the single-operator motion converts.

## Connection to Codex Launch OS

Imjang Companion is not only a product. It is also proof that the launch system can:

- define an ICP
- choose a monetization path
- ship a working vertical product
- carry the product into staging and launch operations

If the vertical app wins, the launch system becomes a second product line.

## What not to optimize for yet

- ad-supported listing traffic
- generalized real-estate portal features
- enterprise collaboration
- OCR-heavy workflows
- broad marketplace referral monetization
