# PRICING.md

## Pricing principle

Pricing in this repository is a hypothesis, not a fact. Validate with paid pilots before automating self-serve billing.

## Offer ladder

### 1. Buyer Project Pack

- type: one-time purchase
- target: individual buyer or couple evaluating one purchase cycle
- packaging: single workspace, limited duration, report export, photo storage
- hypothesis range: KRW 39,000 to KRW 99,000

### 2. Solo Pro

- type: recurring monthly subscription
- target: independent buyer agent or solo investor
- packaging: unlimited projects, report sharing, attachment storage, comparison history
- hypothesis range: KRW 99,000 to KRW 149,000 per month

### 3. Team Workspace

- type: recurring monthly team subscription
- target: small investor team or boutique agency
- packaging: multiple operators, shared workspace, role-based review, branded output
- hypothesis range: KRW 299,000 to KRW 599,000 per month

### 4. Concierge or white-label add-on

- type: custom scope
- target: high-touch pilots
- packaging: migration help, branded reports, launch support, custom workflow
- pricing: quote-based

## Packaging rules

- do not hide the core value inside a free plan forever
- make report sharing and structured comparison part of the paid value
- keep consumer packaging short-lived and transaction-shaped
- use team and brand controls as B2B upsell levers

## Validation sequence

1. run customer interviews on willingness to pay
2. sell a manual or concierge pilot before self-serve billing
3. validate whether one-time buyer packs convert better than low-end subscriptions
4. only add annual plans after several monthly renewals exist

## Metrics that matter

- interview to paid pilot conversion
- paid pilot to monthly subscription conversion
- number of completed visits per paying account
- number of reports shared per account
- first report generation time after signup

## Pricing risks

- pricing too low makes the product look like a note-taking utility
- pricing too high without report-quality proof will stall pilots
- selling consumers on subscription instead of transaction-shaped value will hurt retention
